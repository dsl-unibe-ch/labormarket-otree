Worker Skill Level and Training

Worker skill levels, productivity and employer revenue

Recall that an employer's revenue depends on both the worker's chosen effort level and the worker's skill level:

The higher the worker's skill level, the higher the employer's revenue for any level of worker's effort. This is because a higher worker's skill level is associated with a higher worker's productivity.

However, as a worker's skill level increases, the additional worker's productivity and employer's revenue gained from each increase in skill becomes smaller. For example, increasing a worker's skill level from 1 to 2 boosts productivity from 100 to 140 (an increase of 40), while increasing the skill level from 5 to 6 only raises productivity from 242 to 270 (an increase of 28).
