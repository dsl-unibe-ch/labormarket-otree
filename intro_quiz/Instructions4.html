Worker skill levels, productivity and employer revenue

Recall that an employer's revenue depends on both the worker's chosen effort level and the worker's skill level:

The higher the worker's skill level, the higher the employer's revenue for any level of worker's effort. This is because a higher worker's skill level is associated with a higher worker's productivity.

However, as a worker's skill level increases, the additional worker's productivity and employer's revenue gained from each increase in skill level becomes smaller. {{ example }}
