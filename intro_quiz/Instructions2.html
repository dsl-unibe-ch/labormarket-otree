In this study, employers and workers act in a labor market with six employers and six workers. This study consists of 10 periods in total. Each period will be identical. Every period consists of two parts: (1) The hiring phase and (2) the work phase.

Hiring Phase

The hiring phase consists of several steps in which employers and workers interact to form contracts. That is, employers can make contract offers to workers and workers can decide whether to accept a contract offer.

Employer makes contract offers

In each period, employers start with reviewing the workers. Information about workers includes:

  • each worker's color alias, and
  • each worker's skill level (as described in more detail below).

Then, each employer has to decide whether to:

  • Make a contract offer to one worker from the six potential workers on the labor market, or
  • Not make any contract offer in that period.

A contract offer includes the following information about what will be offered by the employer to the worker in that period:

  • a salary between 1 and 1500 points, and
  • whether or not training will be provided (as discussed in more detail below).

Importantly, employers must fulfill the salary and training specified in the contract, if the contract offer is accepted by a worker.

Workers decide whether to accept a contract offer

Next, workers will be provided with a list of all contract offers they received in this period. It is possible that a worker does not receive any offer, receives one offer, or receives multiple offers.

Each worker has to decide whether to:

  • Accept one of the contract offers, or
  • Decline all contract offers.

Employers and workers that form a contract will be matched for the subsequent work phase of the period.

Unmatched employers and workers

Employers and workers that do not form a contract remain on the labor market.

Employers can decide to:

  • select a different worker to make a contract offer to, or
  • not make an offer for that period.

Importantly, an employer cannot make an offer to a worker that has already rejected a contract offer from this employer in this period. That means, if the worker rejects the offer, the employer can make an offer to one of the other workers he/she has not attempted to hire yet and that are still available on the market but cannot make more than one offer to the same worker in the same period.

The process will continue until either all employers and workers form contracts and are matched or until the remaining (unmatched) employers and workers cannot agree on a contract. In case the remaining employers and workers cannot agree on a contract, they will miss out on the work phase for this period and only receive the initial endowment as payment for that period (described below).
