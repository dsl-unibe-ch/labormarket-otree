Work Phase

Employers and workers that form a contract in the hiring phase continue to the work phase. In this phase, workers decide how much effort, from level 1 to 10, to exert for the employer.

Worker costs based on worker effort level

  • Effort is costly to the worker, such that higher effort levels chosen by the worker increase the worker's costs.

Employer revenue based on a worker's effort level and a worker's skill level

  • Employer revenue depends on both a worker's chosen effort level and a worker's skill level.
  • A worker's skill level determines a worker's productivity. The higher a worker's skill level, the higher the worker's productivity and thus the higher the employer's revenues, for any given effort level. Specifically: Employer revenue = worker effort level Ã— worker productivity. For example, if a worker with skill level 1 chooses effort level 4, employer revenue = 100 x 4 = 400, but if a worker with skill level 5 chooses effort level 4, employer revenue = 242 x 4 = 968.
  • As you can see from the table below, for every worker's skill level, the higher the effort level the worker chooses, the higher the employer's revenues. For example, if a worker with a skill level of 1 chooses effort level 4, employer revenue = 100 x 4 = 400, but if a worker with a skill level of 1 chooses effort level 8, employer revenue = 100 x 8 = 800.
  • A worker's skill level can be increased if the worker receives training (as discussed below).
